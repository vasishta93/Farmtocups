Brew Guide

Cold Brew

In recent years, cold brew coffee has surged in popularity, and for good reason. This brewing method produces a smooth, less acidic, and highly caffeinated coffee that's perfect for hot summer days or for those who prefer a gentler coffee experience. Unlike traditional iced coffee, which is brewed hot and then cooled, cold brew is steeped in cold water for an extended period, resulting in a uniquely flavored concentrate. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of making delicious cold brew coffee at home.

What You'll Need:

  1. - Coarsely ground coffee beans
  2. - Cold, filtered water
  3. - Large glass jar or pitcher
  4. - Cheesecloth or paper coffee filters
  5. - Fine-mesh sieve
  6. - Storage container for the finished concentrate

Step-by-Step Brewing Guide:

  1. Combine Coffee and Water: Place 100g ground coffee in a large glass jar or pitcher. Slowly add 1000ml (about 5 cups) of cold, filtered water. This 1:0 coffee-to-water ratio will produce a ready to drink cold brew.
  2. Stir Gently: Use a long spoon or spatula to gently stir the mixture, ensuring all the grounds are saturated. This helps to kickstart the extraction process.
  3. Cover and Refrigerate: Cover the container with a lid or plastic wrap. Place it in the refrigerator to steep for 12-24 hours. The longer the steep, the stronger the brew, but be cautious as over-steeping can lead to bitterness.
  4. First Strain: After steeping, strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean container. This removes the larger coffee grounds.
  5. Second Strain: For a cleaner cup, strain the coffee again through a sieve lined with cheesecloth or a paper coffee filter. This step removes any fine sediment.
  6. Store: Transfer your cold brew concentrate to an airtight container and store it in the refrigerator. It will stay fresh for up to 2 weeks.
  7. Serve: Serve over ice for a refreshing cold coffee drink. Additionally if you make a concentrate one can add milk to dilute.

Pro Tips for Cold Brew Perfection

  1. Bean Selection: While any coffee bean can be used, medium to dark roasts often work best for cold brew, providing a richer, chocolatey flavor profile.
  2. Water Quality: Use filtered water for the cleanest taste. The slow extraction process in cold brew makes water quality especially important.
  3. Grind Consistency: A consistent, coarse grind is crucial. Too fine a grind can lead to over-extraction and bitterness.
  4. Experiment with Ratios: The 1:0 ratio produce sa ready-to-drink cold brew, try a 1:5 ratio instead, if you prefer a concentrate.
  5. Steeping Time: 12-24 hours is the sweet spot for most cold brews. Taste your brew at 12 hours and then every few hours to find your perfect steeping time.
  6. Double Filtering: The second filtering step might seem tedious, but it results in a cleaner, smoother cold brew.
  7. Flavor Infusions: Try adding spices like cinnamon sticks or vanilla beans to the grounds before brewing for interesting flavor variations.
  8. Ice Cubes: For a truly undiluted experience,use clear large ice cubes in your drink.



Cold brew coffee offers a unique and refreshing coffee experience that's perfect for warm weather or for those who find traditional coffee too acidic. While it requires more time than other brewing methods, the hands-on effort is minimal, and the result is a smooth, versatile coffee concentrate that can be enjoyed in various ways.

Whether you're sipping it straight over ice, mixing it with your favorite milk, or using it as a base for coffee cocktails, cold brew provides a canvas for creativity in your coffee routine. The beauty of making cold brew at home is the ability to customize every aspect - from the coffee beans you use to the steeping time and the final dilution ratio.

So, as the weather warms up (or even if it doesn't - cold brew is great year-round!), give this method a try. With a little patience and experimentation, you'll be crafting cafe-quality cold brew in the comfort of your own home. Happy brewing, and stay cool!
